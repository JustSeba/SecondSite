Keeping Calm

Doesn't sound like a skill, does it? Communication, teamwork, leadership, now these are skills! (or at least pseudo-skills that you write to fill the empty space in your CV. Granted, there are some rare individuals that actually possess these to a high enough degree to be considered skills). Keeping calm isn't just a passive trait, it's something you have to work at and is more of an active skill then you might first consider. When everything is going up in flames, it takes considerable mental focus to ignore the chaos around you and focus on your work. In today's high-stress environment, being calm is not easy, and it brings numerous benefits to the workplace: the calm person has a higher retention rate, lower absenteeism, is less likely to quit his job or have a breakdown, and can sometimes be the glue that holds everything together when things are falling apart. While I have not mastered the skill, it is something I strive for in day to day life.
